 &#13; Seeds&#13; &#13; In the first phase of the creative process, we are to be completely open, collecting anything we find of interest.&#13; We can call this the Seed phase.  &#13; Momentum&#13; &#13; When treated like the earlier phases, with no boundaries or time constraints, the Craft phase may extend longer than necessary.&#13; Once enough data is collected, and the vision is clear, it can be helpful to set deadlines for completion. The options are no longer unlimited; the process is less open-ended. There may not be a clear finish line in sight, but the core elements are there.&#13; Imagine you have a script that has been translated to storyboard. Our creative task now has narrower parameters.&#13; If we‚Äôre pleased with the blueprint, it could be constructed many different ways. The power is held in the underlying structure.&#13; If the project were a building, we‚Äôre picking which materials to clad it in and what type of windows to install. The details matter, but they aren‚Äôt likely to sink the enterprise.&#13; In the Craft phase, deadlines are suggested completion dates rather than set in stone. The result can be a loss of connection and enthusiasm for the work over time.&#13; Another challenge we might call demo-itis. A musician might record a demo of a song very quickly. They could listen to it thousands of times and imagine developing it to all it can be.  &#13; Art is choosing to do something skillfully,&#13; caring about the details,&#13; bringing all of yourself&#13; to make the finest work you can.&#13; It is beyond ego, vanity, self-glorification,&#13; and need for approval.&#13; &#13;

 &#13; Point of View&#13; &#13; The goal of art isn‚Äôt to attain perfection. The goal is to share who we are. We hold on to stories about our perspective that are inaccurate and limiting.&#13; Wayne Dyer said that when you squeeze an orange, what comes out is orange juice. We are free to be present and allow the material to come through us, and free to stay out of its way when it does come through.&#13; Much of art‚Äôs greatness is felt on a gut level. If your work speaks to them, it is of no consequence if you are heard and understood.&#13; Set aside such concerns about whether your work will be comprehended. And often this conversation is started by accident.&#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; Most human beings like to fit in.&#13; We adapt not only to the evolving flow of material coming through us, but the boundaries and templates of the culture around us.&#13; Can great art come from conformity? And what is the purpose of being an artist if we deny our unique personal point of view?&#13; Those of us choosing to live as artists embrace our filter as a gift. To reject it would be tragic. The refracted light it projects is our own singular landscape of artistic possibility. How can a piece of art ever truly be a guilty pleasure?&#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; The Beatles were inspired by American rock and roll, artists like Chuck Berry and the Shirelles.  &#13; Breaking the Sameness&#13; &#13; There are times during the Craft phase when you hit a wall and the work isn‚Äôt getting any better. Whether or not you find yourself at an impasse, perhaps they can inspire similar experiments in your chosen field.&#13; Small Steps&#13; To create movement for a musician who was blocked, we offered him a small task: write just one line every day. It didn‚Äôt matter how good or bad he felt about the line, as long as he committed to writing it. If more came through, that was fine but not necessary. This happened much more quickly than expected.&#13; Change the Environment&#13; If we‚Äôre looking for a performance of a different nature, it can help to change an element of the environment. If a band imagines that this is the last time they‚Äôll ever play a particular song, they‚Äôre likely to perform it differently than if it‚Äôs just another take. While some artists may overdo a performance for an audience and others may hold back, most tend to be more focused with someone else present. The goal is to find the specific parameters in each case that bring out your best.&#13; Change the Context&#13; There are times when a singer doesn‚Äôt connect with a song, like an actor whose line reading falls flat. It‚Äôs worth experimenting with different scenarios to observe what they bring out and to find the performance you want.&#13; Write for Someone Else&#13; In the case of a musician who typically writes their own material, I‚Äôll suggest, ‚ÄúImagine that a favorite artist asked you to write a song for their next album. A quintessential song of female empowerment, ‚Äú(You Make Me Feel Like) A Natural Woman‚Äù was written by Carole King and Gerry Goffin. King‚Äîand then, of course, Aretha Franklin‚Äîsang it.  &#13; Completion&#13; &#13; As the work improves through the Craft phase, you‚Äôll come to the point where all of the options available to you have been explored sufficiently.&#13; The seed has achieved its full expression and you‚Äôve pruned it to your satisfaction.  &#13; The Abundant Mindset&#13; &#13; A river of material flows through us. When we share our works and our ideas, they are replenished.  &#13; The Experimenter and the Finisher&#13; &#13; In their nature, many artists lean toward one of two categories: Experimenters or Finishers.&#13; Experimenters are partial to dreaming and play, finding it more difficult to complete and release their work.&#13; Finishers are the mirror image, a backward reflection.  &#13; How to Choose&#13; &#13; Every piece of art consists of a series of choices, like a tree with many branches.&#13; Our work begins with a seed, which sprouts a trunk of the core idea.  &#13; Clean Slate&#13; &#13; After spending thousands of hours working on a piece, it‚Äôs difficult to judge it from a neutral place.  &#13; The Prism of Self&#13; &#13; Defining one‚Äôs true self is not so simple. It may be impossible.&#13; We inhabit many different versions of a changing self.  &#13; Any framework, method, or label&#13; you impose on yourself&#13; is just as likely to be a limitation&#13; as an opening.&#13; &#13;

 &#13; Let It Be&#13; &#13; First, do no harm.&#13; This credo is the well-known guiding principle of the physician‚Äôs oath. Consider it a universal precept.  &#13; Cooperation&#13; &#13; The prism of self reflects an aspect of our being into our work. When more than one prism is applied, unexpected possibilities can be unlocked. Whether the perspectives contrast or complement each other, they combine to create a new vision.&#13; Let‚Äôs call this Cooperation.&#13; Like awareness, cooperation is a practice. The more skillfully we participate in the process, the more comfortable it becomes.&#13; Cooperation is comparable to the way a jazz ensemble improvises. You are not compromising. You are working together to surpass the current iteration.&#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; We might not create equally with every partner. It‚Äôs bringing us closer to the best version of the work at hand.&#13; Some collaborations operate more like dictatorships than democracies. This system can work as well. In these cases, everyone agrees to line up behind one person‚Äôs vision and do all they can to manifest it.&#13; Whether the final decision is made by a single leader or a collective, it‚Äôs still a collaborative act. The participants are offering up their best work in the spirit of cooperation.&#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; &#13; Communication is the core of skillful cooperation.&#13; When giving feedback, don‚Äôt make it personal. Always comment on the work itself and not the individual who made it. If a participant takes a critique personally, they tend to shut down.&#13; Be as specific as possible with your feedback.  &#13; The synergy of a group&#13; is as important‚Äî&#13; if not more important‚Äî&#13; than the talent&#13; of the individuals.&#13; &#13;

 &#13; The Sincerity Dilemma&#13; &#13; Most artists overvalue sincerity.&#13; They strive to create art that expresses their truth. The truest version of themselves.&#13; Sincerity, however, is an elusive characteristic. It is different from other goals we may have.  &#13; Anything that allows the audience&#13; to access how you see the world&#13; is accurate,&#13; even if the information is wrong.&#13; &#13;

 &#13; The Gatekeeper&#13; &#13; No matter where your ideas come from or what they look like, they all eventually pass through a particular aspect of yourself: the editor, the gatekeeper.&#13; This is who will determine the final expression of the work, regardless of how many selves were involved in its construction.&#13; The editor‚Äôs role is to gather and sift.  &#13; Why Make Art?&#13; &#13; As you deepen your participation in the creative act, you may come across a paradox.&#13; Ultimately, the act of self-expression isn‚Äôt really about you.&#13; Most who choose the artist‚Äôs path don‚Äôt have a choice.  &#13; Harmony&#13; &#13; The invisible threads of mathematics are laced through all natural beauty.&#13; We can find the same ratios at work in the spirals of seashells and galaxies.
