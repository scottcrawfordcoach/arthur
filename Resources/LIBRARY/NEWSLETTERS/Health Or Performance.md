HEALTH or PERFORMANCE?
Hi Scott,
In Simon Sinek's book "The Infinite Game," he outlines the idea that we often, as individuals
and as a society, approach processes in a finite way.
In that, we approach it like a sports game, where there are set rules, a defined playing area,
and the point of the game is to WIN - trying to be RIGHT.
Unfortunately, many of the aspects of our lives are more chaotic - they have non-defined
playing areas and rules, and people drop in and out of the game randomly - an infinite game.
The point of an infinite game is to PLAY FOR AS LONG AS WE CAN.
Education, Business, Politics, Friendship, Health, Marriage.
These are all infinite games.
If we treat these as a zero sum game with a winner and a loser, we might 'win' today, but find
that eventually, no-one wants to play with us any more. Or we're in too much pain to go on.
Most of us are probably familiar with the idea of SMART goals (Specific, Measurable,
Achievable, Relevant, and Time-Bound), but before we get to setting SMART goals or any

When it comes to fitness, if our goal is PERFORMANCE, then we start with a very single-
purposed position on how the various aspects of our lives are balanced. We put winning
ahead of almost all else. Performance goals tend to be part of a finite game.
If our goal is to be HEALTHY, then we need to take a much more measured and balanced
approach, and appreciate that our health has different components, which all contribute to our
overall wellness. Health goals tend to be part of an infinite game.
My focus is really on Health goals.
Of course we can punctuate a health journey with mini finite goals and milestones along the
way, but always keep a focus on a holistic view and the need to live to fight another day.
It's a dance between accepting that we will not be able to bring our full power to every
session, but also developing robust, disciplined habits that sustain us through periods of low
energy and motivation.
