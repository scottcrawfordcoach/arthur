If you know that you are chronically under-rested, and you have the chance to catch up on
sleep, then it might be best to take advantage of that opportunity. However, if you are awake
anyway, tossing and turning, then the chances are, it‚Äôs going to be worth showing up to the

We need to be sensible and realistic about our exercise choices on a day following poor

sleep deprived is a good way to invite illness, pain, or reduced motivation to show up next
time.
My recommendation after a bad sleep is to still exercise, at your usual time if you can, and be
sure to use that as a recovery day - focus on healthy movement, rather than heavy lifting - it‚Äôs
not the time for setting records.
